# Skill: Education — Early-Career Candidate Guidelines

## Overview
For early-career candidates (recent graduates, candidates with 0–3 years of professional experience, or career changers entering a new field), the education section carries significantly more weight than for experienced candidates. This guide explains when and how to promote education, and what to include to maximize its impact.

---

## When These Rules Apply

Apply early-career education rules when ANY of the following is true:
- `experience_level` is `"Entry"` or `"Junior"`
- The candidate has fewer than 3 years of professional experience
- The candidate is a recent graduate (graduated within the last 2 years)
- The candidate is making a career change and their new-field experience is limited
- The experience section has fewer than 2 entries, or existing entries are internships/part-time roles only

---

## Section Placement

For experienced candidates: Education appears after Experience.

**For early-career candidates: Education should appear BEFORE or AT THE SAME LEVEL as Experience** — potentially even before Projects, depending on the prestige of the institution or relevance of the degree to the target role.

Placement decision logic:
1. If the degree is directly relevant to the role (e.g., CS degree for a Software Engineer role, Statistics degree for a Data Scientist role): **Place Education before Experience**
2. If the institution is highly recognized in the field: **Place Education before Experience**
3. If neither of the above: **Place Education after Projects but before less-relevant Experience**

---

## What to Include in the Education Section (Early-Career)

### Always include
- Institution name (official name, not abbreviation unless well-known)
- Degree name (full form: "Bachelor of Science", not "B.S.")
- Field of study / major
- Graduation year (or expected graduation month + year if currently enrolled)

### Include when applicable

#### GPA
Include GPA if: 3.5 or above on a 4.0 scale (or equivalent honor class for international degrees).

Format: "GPA: 3.8/4.0" — always show the denominator.

Do not include if below 3.5, unless the job posting explicitly asks for it.

**International equivalents**:
| Country | Include if... |
|---|---|
| UK/Australia | First Class Honours or Upper Second Class (2:1) |
| Germany | Note "1.x" (German scale inverted) — convert to "Excellent/Very Good (German scale: 1.3)" |
| India | CGPA ≥ 8.0/10.0 or "Distinction" class |

#### Relevant Coursework
Include when the target role requires specific technical knowledge that is not yet evidenced in the experience section.

List 4–8 courses by their recognized short name. Group them thematically if more than 6.

Example for a Software Engineer role:
> **Relevant Coursework**: Data Structures & Algorithms, Operating Systems, Database Systems, Computer Networks, Machine Learning, Distributed Systems

Example for a Data Scientist role:
> **Relevant Coursework**: Statistical Inference, Machine Learning, Linear Algebra, Probability Theory, Data Mining, Bayesian Statistics

Rules:
- Only include courses demonstrably relevant to the target role
- Use short names that match industry terminology (not the university's internal catalog names)
- Do not list courses that are generic requirements (English Composition, Physical Education)
- If the role requires "strong CS fundamentals", prioritize: Data Structures, Algorithms, Systems, OS, Networks

#### Honors and Awards
Include academic distinctions that signal exceptional performance:
- Dean's List (note number of semesters if multiple: "Dean's List — 6/8 semesters")
- Graduated with Honors / Magna Cum Laude / Summa Cum Laude
- Departmental awards or prizes
- Competitive scholarships (especially merit-based or nationally competitive ones)
- National exam rankings (relevant in some countries)

Format: List as bullet points under the institution entry, or as a separate "Awards" sub-line.

#### Thesis or Capstone Project
Include when:
- The thesis topic is directly relevant to the target role
- The research demonstrates advanced technical skills (e.g., implemented a novel ML model, built a production-grade system)
- The thesis was at a graduate level (Master's or PhD thesis is high-value for technical roles)

What to include:
- Thesis title
- 1-sentence description of the methodology or outcome
- Advisor name (optional, only for prestigious advisor or well-known research group)
- Publication or link (if published)

Example:
> **Thesis**: "Real-Time Anomaly Detection in Financial Transaction Streams" — Implemented a streaming LSTM model using PyTorch and Apache Kafka, achieving 94.2% F1 on a synthetic dataset; advised by Prof. [Name], [Lab/Group Name]

#### Study Abroad
Include if:
- The program was at a prestigious institution
- The coursework is directly relevant
- It demonstrates language skills or international experience relevant to the role (e.g., global company, international team)

Format: "Exchange Program — [Institution], [Country] ([Semester/Year])"

#### Extracurricular Activities Relevant to the Role
For technical roles, include activities that demonstrate applied skills:
- Coding competitions: "ICPC Regional Finalist", "HackerRank Top 500 in [year]"
- Hackathons: "Winner, [Hackathon Name] — Built [brief description]"
- Technical clubs: "President, ACM Student Chapter", "Lead, Machine Learning Society"
- Research assistant work (if not listed as experience): "Research Assistant, [Lab], [Year]"
- Teaching assistant work: "Teaching Assistant, Data Structures (CS 201), 3 semesters"

---

## What NOT to Include

Regardless of career stage, do not include:

- High school / secondary education (unless no college degree and the role requires it)
- GPA below 3.5 (it hurts more than it helps)
- Irrelevant coursework (every course listed should pass the "would a recruiter care?" test)
- Generic activities (intramural sports, unless you are applying to a sports-adjacent company)
- Transfer institution (unless the institution is notable — just list the final degree-granting institution)

---

## Education Section Length Guidance

| Career Stage | Recommended Length |
|---|---|
| Recent grad (0–1 year) | 6–10 lines (full detail: GPA, coursework, honors, activities) |
| Early career (1–3 years) | 4–6 lines (GPA, key coursework, honors only) |
| Mid career (3–7 years) | 2–3 lines (institution, degree, year — no coursework) |
| Senior+ (7+ years) | 1–2 lines (institution and degree name only) |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Listing coursework that is standard for every CS student**: "Introduction to Programming", "Discrete Mathematics" — these add no signal; every CS applicant took them.
- **Not listing GPA when it is strong**: A 3.9 GPA that is hidden is a missed differentiator.
- **Using the university's internal course code instead of the name**: "6.046" means nothing to a recruiter outside MIT; always include the human-readable name.
- **Omitting a relevant thesis**: A thesis on a directly relevant topic is one of the strongest differentiators for early-career technical candidates.
- **Listing too many activities**: 3–4 high-quality activities are more impactful than 10 generic ones.
